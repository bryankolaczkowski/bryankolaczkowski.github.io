NSF AI Across the Curriculum FAQ

What is "NSF AI Across the Curriculum"?

NSF AI Across the Curriculum is a joint program at FAMU and UF, funded by the US National Science Foundation (NSF). The goal of this program is to enhance the diversity of undergraduate students pursuing AI-related coursework. AI is poised to redefine nearly all aspects of human society. We want to help create a future workforce in which diverse viewpoints are included and AI systems are developed and deployed equitably and fairly. We think the best way to do this is to ensure that diverse students enter the workforce equipped to engage AI in their chosen field. To support these goals, our program provides direct financial assistance to undergraduate students pursuing AI-related coursework and undergraduate research and scholarship.

What is AI?

AI or "Artifiical Intelligence" can be defined as any approach that attempts to 'mimic' human or other 'natural intelligence' using computers. Our program defines "AI" very broadly and includes not only traditional computer-science work but also critique of AI, examinations of AI's impact on society, law and the natural world, AI ethics, applications of AI in the sciences, humanities and business, and artistic works that incorporate AI.

Who is eligible for undergraduate awards?

Any undergraduate student attending FAMU or UF and majoring in any discipline is eligible for awards through our program. If you want to learn to engate AI in your field, we want to support you!

How do I apply for an undergraduate award?

Details about the available undergraduate awards can be found on our main awards page. Award applications can be submitted by filling out a short online form.

What is the deadline for undergraduate award applications?

There is no deadline for any of our undergraduate awards; you can apply at any time. Awards will be made on a rolling basis, and it typically takes us about 1 month to approve and process a successful award application. We will continue to process and approve awards unitil we run out of money. We typically receive funding from NSF in the early fall of each year, so it's probably best to apply for awards during late summer, before the start of the fall semester.

If I receive an award, how do I get my money?

Undergraduate awards will be deposited directly into your student account at your university, subject to university policies and regulations. In most cases, awards will be applied toward any outstanding tuition and fees at your university. Any remainder is typically returned to you as a check, although this is subject to your university's policies.

Are there reporting requirements for undergraduate awards?

Nope. After you receive an award, you don't need to report your activities to us, unless you want to. Work with your academic or faculty advisor at your university to fulfill the requirements of your award.

What happens if I can't complete the requirements of my award?

We understand that various hardships can come up. Our awards are flexible and don't have strict timelines. If you are having difficulty fulfilling the requirements of your award, you should first work with your academic or faculty advisor at your university to try to get back on track. If you have any serious concerns, please contact us through the contact form at the bottom of this page, and we will work with you to help address your concerns. And don't panic, we aren't going to track you down and try to get your award money back! We're here to help you succeed, not cause more difficulty.
